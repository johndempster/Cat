The heart and its receptors

 

 

The vagus nerve releases acetylcholine and acts via muscarinic cholinoceptors (mAChR) on the heart to slow heart rate and reduce cardiac force.

 

The baroreceptor reflex. Baroreceptors within the CNS when stimulated by high arterial presssure increase the nerve activity along the vagus depressing heart rate and force

 

The accelerans nerves releases noradrenaline and acts via Beta-adrenoceptors (bAdr) on the heart to increase heart rate and contractile force.

 

Both the vagus and accelerans nerves act indirectly via ganglia. Synaptic transmissi. at the ganglia is by neuronal nicotinic cholinoceptors (nAChR).

 

Mu-Opioid and adenosine (A1) receptors are present on the presynaptic nerve endings of both the vagus and accelerans nerves and act to depress transmitter release.

 

Heart muscle also has adenosine (A1) receptors which cause a reduction in heart rate and force in response to circulating adenosine.
